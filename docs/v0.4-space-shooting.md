# v0.4 — Game 1: Space Shooting

**Goal:** The first fully playable game, and the engine's real-world proof. Space
Shooting is the simplest of the three (horizontal player, single bullet, descending
enemies), so it's built first to shake out the v0.2 engine and v0.3 shell.

## Route & files

- Route: `app/shooting/`
- Logic: `games/shooting/`

## Gameplay

- Player ship (**3×2 cells**) moves **horizontally** along the bottom row.
- Enemies (**2×2 or 3×2 cells**) descend from the top in simple patterns
  (e.g. step down, sweep sideways) — no AI, no easing.
- **Single projectile on screen at a time** — can't fire again until the bullet
  resolves (authentic LCD limitation).
- Bullet moves one cell per tick, straight up.
- Grid-based collision: bullet vs enemy = kill + score; enemy reaching the bottom or
  touching the ship = lose a life.
- **Speeds up over time** / per level (faster ticks + spawn rate via difficulty).

## Integrates

- Engine loop/grid/collision/input from v0.2.
- Store: score, level, lives, game-over from v0.2.
- Shell + score panel from v0.3 (mode label = "SPACE").
- Sound hooks are stubbed here and wired for real in v0.7 (shoot/hit/level-up/
  game-over call sites should exist as no-ops).

## Out of scope

- Multiple simultaneous bullets, power-ups, particle explosions (forbidden).

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] Ship moves cell-by-cell horizontally; bounded to the field.
- [ ] Exactly one bullet allowed on screen at a time.
- [ ] Enemies spawn, descend, and collide on the grid correctly.
- [ ] Score/level/lives update; game-over triggers and offers restart.
- [ ] Difficulty ramps (speed + spawn rate).
- [ ] All rendering stays within the 4-color LCD rule.
